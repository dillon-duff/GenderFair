Non-Profit Organization Rankings

Gender Fair’s mission is fairness; we promote fairness in workplaces and institutions. We rate public companies and colleges, and now provide that service for nonprofits. The nonprofit sector provides important services in health care, education, communities, the arts and the environment, etc. With nearly 1.5 million nonprofits (501 C3s) in the United States, the sector makes up a significant piece of the economy: representing $2.6 trillion dollars and employing 7-10% of the US workforce. It’s well known that the sector has not reached gender balance when it comes to leadership or pay. Nor does the sector’s workforce represent the diversity of the American population.

That’s why Gender Fair provides these ratings to show how individual nonprofits perform on gender and diversity. Donors and funding bodies can tell organizations they support that they expect more. Employees can use these ratings to advocate for better workplaces.

Our nonprofit ratings of 20,000 organizations are built from two databases from which we/computer scientists pulled/scraped the data without any human or journalistic data collection. Our sources were IRS 990 filings and Candid. The parameters and size of the two databases and unavailability of human labor necessitated a reduction in the categories which we rate. Nonprofits are rated within three categories, leadership, pay equality, and diversity.

These metrics differ from our ratings of colleges and companies, which use metrics based on the UN Women Empowerment Principles in five different categories: Leadership, Employee Policies, Advertising, Diversity Reporting and Social Impact (LEADS). There is no public database on employee policies, advertising doesn’t pertain to all nonprofits, and social impact is embedded in the sector which narrowed our areas of study.

Certainly, the organizations that seek to improve society can be urged to promote more fairness, more gender balance, and diversity in their workforces. It can start with more public reporting of demographics as well as adding and disclosing workplace policies such as parental leave, hybrid work, mental health benefits, or pay studies etc that create a healthy work atmosphere. Everyone deserves that.
